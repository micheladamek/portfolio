Case Study № 03 — EdTech

GAPMINDER.

Upgrading the world's worldview,one question at a time.

Client — Gapminder Foundation Role — UX Design Lead
Scope Worldview Upgrader — Web App
Foundation Co-founded by Anna & Ola Rosling
Recognition Svenska Designpriset — Nominee 2021
The Brief

A digital extension of the bestseller Factfulness: present statistics about global development in a way that's so much fun, people choose to test their worldview — and discover how wrong it is.

The Gapminder Foundation — co-founded by Anna and Ola Rosling — fights global misconceptions with data. The Worldview Upgrader brings that mission to the web: a game-influenced app where you answer questions about the UN Sustainability Goals, see how your answers compare with reality, and upgrade your worldview fact by fact.

The challenge wasn't the data — Gapminder has the world's best. It was the framing: facts about global development have to compete with everything else on the internet. The product had to feel like play, not homework.

The Process

From paper prototypes to coded prototypes to a thousand real users before launch — every game mechanic earned its place through testing.

Iterate cheap, then real. The work started with extensive market research and rounds of user interviews, then moved through paper prototypes into coded ones — raising the fidelity only when an idea had survived the previous round.

A thousand users before day one. Pre-launch, the app was tested with 1,000 users, and their feedback folded back into the design continuously until the autumn 2020 launch. The result launched proven, not hopeful.

The Results

Measured in millions

31.2M Questions answered
1.8M Users
334K Certificates earned

Figures as of October 2023. Along the way: a nomination for App of the Year at Svenska Designpriset 2021 — and a mention in a Bill Gates tweet, which is not a KPI but absolutely counts.

"By becoming aware of our ignorance, we can learn to be more careful before we claim to have knowledge of things without checking the facts first."

Anna Rosling Rönnlund — Gapminder Foundation
