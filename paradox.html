Case Study № 07 — Gaming

PARADOX INTERACTIVE.

A digital experience that feels likeyou're already in the game.

Client — Paradox Interactive Role — UX Design, Paradox Mods
Scope Paradox Mods · Design System · Visual Expression
Client Stockholm publisher of historical strategy games
Collaborators Product, Engineering — and Sony & Microsoft
The Brief

Mods site, store, launcher — Paradox's digital world had grown one touchpoint at a time. The mission: make it feel like one universe, with an interface worthy of the games.

Paradox Interactive's players are devoted — the kind who mod their favorite game's characters and rewrite its story. But the digital surfaces around the games had fragmented: the mods website, the Paradox Plaza store and the launcher each going their own way. The goal was consistency with personality — a futuristic interface that feels like you're in a game.

My focus was the Paradox Mods experience: its UX, its visual expression, and the design system underneath. The work ran through workshops and user testing — notably around Playsets, a feature letting players manage groups of mods together — in collaboration with product owners, developers, and international stakeholders including Sony and Microsoft.

The Work

Three moves

  • 01

    Paradox Mods

    The website experience for discovering and managing mods — designed to feel like an extension of the games themselves.

  • 02

    Playsets

    User-tested feature design for managing groups of mods — taming complexity players had been handling by hand.

  • 03

    Design System

    A Sketch symbols library and templates standardizing the visual language across touchpoints.

The Results

One universe

  • A cohesive experience across Paradox's previously fragmented touchpoints
  • A design system that made design work faster and new-designer onboarding easier
  • Playsets shipped with player testing behind every decision
