Case Study № 04 — Service Design

STIM.

Research that became a room —a physical home for music creators.

Client — Stim Role — UX Research & Service Design
Client Stim — 90,000+ affiliated rightsholders, est. 1923
Deliverable Stim Music Room — co-working & studios
Recognition Winner — Design S, Service Design 2020
The Brief

How does a 100-year-old rights organization become an indispensable friend to up-and-coming artists? We went and asked the artists.

Stim has compensated music creators since 1923 — today it represents more than 90,000 rightsholders and publishers. But collecting royalties is a quiet kind of relationship. Stim wanted a closer one: to understand the needs and behaviors of emerging artists, and to matter to them earlier in their careers.

The approach was classic UX with an open mind about the outcome: interviews with artists, effect mapping and idea-generation workshops with the client, user profiles and customer journey maps. Nobody decided in advance that the answer would be digital. It wasn't.

The Turning Point

The research pointed somewhere unexpected: what emerging artists lacked wasn't another app — it was a place.

The insight. Up-and-coming creators needed studio access, industry knowledge and each other — things a screen handles poorly and a room handles beautifully.

The decision. Stim renovated a floor of its Stockholm headquarters into the Stim Music Room: a free co-working space for songwriters, composers and publishers, with two recording studios, industry know-how on tap, and the networking that happens when you put creative people in the same room.

The Results

An award-winning room

  • Stim Music Room opened — free studios, knowledge and community for members
  • Winner of Design S, the Swedish Design Awards, in Service Design 2020
  • Member research turned into practical, value-creating solutions — not another report

"Stim Music Room shows the importance of physical meeting places in a digital world."

From the project — and the thesis of the whole case
